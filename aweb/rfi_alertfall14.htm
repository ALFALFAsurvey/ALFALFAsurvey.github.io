RFI alert for Aug, Sep, Dec 2014

We have received updated notice from the US Space Command (USSC),
Colorado Springs, CO, informing the RA community of more detail
in the schedule for the on-orbit checkout of the NDT capabilities
of the new GPS 2F-7 SV.  (The GPS 2F-7 launch is currently scheduled
for 201407305.)  These tests trigger GPS satellites over the central
U.S., but may propagate throughout the entire GPS constellation.  The
observatories should expect increased RFI @ 1381 MHz during the dates
& times indicated below.

(These extended-duration tests are in addition to the regular, daily
short-duration (< 180 sec/day) GPS-L3 tests allowed for in the USAF/
NSF MOU.)

VLA & VLBA schedulers should attempt to schedule 1381 MHz use around
these times if possible.  Observing scientists should be made aware of
the increased possibility of corrupted data on these dates and times.
VLA and VLBA operators should note unusual L-band Tsys or total power
fluctuations observed during these times in the Operator Log forwarded
to observers.

Please contact me for coordination should any of these dates and times
conflict with planned radio astronomy observations.

-Mert
